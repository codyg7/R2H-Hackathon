Who We Are

Our Mission

1)Our mission is to change the lives of those who are facing economic hardship become financially stable by providing free financial education.

2)To educate our youth in Financial Responsibility so that they never have to deal with economic hardships.

About us

Money is one factor that stresses just about everyone. Some economic hardships even cause people to become homeless, cause depression, and sadly suicide. We strongly believe that people who face financial hardships are there because they lack the knowledge on how to become financially stable. People don't know how to budget and save, how to build credit and how to properly use it, or how to get out of debt. By teaching financial literacy we believe that we can change our community for the better by taking the stress of money out of our peoples lives.

Financial literacy can be taught in many forms and there are a variety of resources we provide. We have weekly classes and seminars that people can attend. Some of our seminar topics consist of credit repair, budgeting and saving, and filing and preparing taxes. We work with local companies and volunteers to organize career fairs in order to help those who are unemployed get employed. If you are facing economic hardships we also have financial advisors, accountants, brokers,and tax preparers who volunteer there time to provide advice and can help you get into a better place.

Manuel Jimenez

Manuel Jimenez is an honest hardworking man who worked in construction all his life to provide for his family. Unfortunately one day at work he fell from the roof and broke his leg. He did not have any type medical insurance to cover the hospital expenses. When the bill came in the mail it was a little over $80,000 that he was in debt for. Due to his injury he was not able to return to work anytime soon and had lost his home . He came to us with and one of our volunteer lawyers advices him to sue his employer for improper equipment and not following OSHA regulations. He won the case and with the help from our classes and financial advisors the money he received he used to start his own business and purchased a new home for his family and himself. Manuel now volunteers by telling his story and helps pass his knowledge to help those facing economic hardships.
